## 2. Methodology

I approached this as a career strategist answering: "Where should a 24-year-old Turkish software engineer with 22 months left on a UK Graduate visa, a flagship Rust portfolio, and a secular-modernist lifestyle profile actually plant himself for the highest career-trajectory expected value over 10 years?"

My priorities, in rough order:

1. **The August 2027 clock dominates everything.** Every recommendation has to survive the question: can this candidate actually be working there on 1 September 2027? Hope-based strategies do not count.
2. **Firm density and graduate pipeline depth** in Caner's exact target sectors (HFT/quant, fintech infra, AI infra, systems, Rust-in-production) are the career ceiling for the next 5 years. I weighted these very heavily.
3. **Lifestyle fit** matters but I refused to let it veto a city that dominates on career + visa. Caner himself said his early 20s are for career, lifestyle second. I took that at face value.
4. **Safety and political trajectory** I weighted hard because the candidate is leaving Turkey partly to escape exactly those failures. Don't swap one authoritarian-drift country for another.
5. **Trajectory over current state.** I asked "where is this city in 2033, 2041?" not just "what does it look like today?"

I used WebSearch and WebFetch to verify 2026 visa thresholds, salary floors, hiring conditions, nightlife decline trends, and Waymo's confirmed London launch — my training data cuts off in May 2025 and blindly trusting it would embarrass me on anything post-cutoff.

My angle that distinguishes this report from the other 9 agents running in parallel: I am explicitly willing to **overrule the profile's Amsterdam rejection** because I think the career EV from Optiver/IMC/Flow Traders is so extreme that it justifies 2–3 years of aesthetic friction, and because the body-scaling concern is cosmetic in a way that "ulcerating unsafe Croydon nights" is not. I am also unusually bullish on **London staying put** — the 2027 deadline can be pushed with Skilled Worker sponsorship at any of the >200 firms Cernio has already identified with London offices and sponsor licences. Leaving London before you have to leave London is almost always the wrong call for this profile.

## 3. Continent-by-continent sweep

### 3a. Europe

Europe is Caner's gravity well. He is in London already. Most of the good answers are within 3 hours' flight. Continental Europe has two things the UK can't match: the Blue Card path, which is a structurally gentler visa regime, and a set of specific firms (Dutch HFTs, Paris AI labs, Swiss quant offices) that are not replicated in the UK. Europe also has what the UK does not: clear PR paths measured in single-digit years rather than five + indefinite leave to remain with £41,700 sponsorship floors.

#### United Kingdom

- **London** — Caner's current base and, after four hours of research, still his best single bet. Detail in the deep dive below. Key 2026 facts: Skilled Worker threshold rose to £41,700 on 22 July 2025, with occupation-specific going rates on top. Software engineering SOC codes clear this at mid-to-senior band but mid-graduate roles at some firms may be tight against it. Waymo confirmed London launch for 2026. The Kings Cross / Nine Elms / Battersea / White City regenerated-district axis that Caner explicitly calls his aesthetic gold standard is not being dismantled — it is being extended. The firm density is SS-tier: Jane Street, HRT, Citadel Securities, Stripe, DeepMind, Anthropic (London office), Graphcore (Bristol with London satellites), Thought Machine, Palantir, Cloudflare, Monzo, Revolut. The graduate pipeline is the deepest in Europe outside arguably NYC. **Verdict: stay.**
- **Cambridge** — Worth mentioning only for Graphcore, ARM, Riverlane, Speechmatics. Aesthetic and lifestyle are weaker than London (historic university town, limited mixed-scale regenerated districts, café culture is small-scale), but if a specific AI hardware or compiler role lands there, it clears the rubric because of firm-specific density. Treat as a bolt-on to a London search, not as an independent target.
- **Manchester / Edinburgh / Leeds** — Skip. Not a meaningful answer for this profile. Firm density in Caner's target sectors is thin, aesthetic is stronger in Edinburgh but lifestyle integration is weaker, and Manchester's safety and aesthetic both underwhelm relative to London's regenerated districts.

#### Ireland

- **Dublin** — **Real answer.** The Critical Skills Employment Permit was restructured for 1 March 2026 and the thresholds are €40,904 general and **€36,848 for new-graduate roles in Critical Skills Occupation List jobs**, which explicitly includes software developers. Stripe's Dublin new-grad SWE base is advertised at €64k–€96k, which clears any CSEP threshold with room. The move to Stamp 4 (PR equivalent) after 2 years is dramatically faster than UK indefinite leave to remain. Aesthetic is the weak axis — Dublin is mostly Georgian low-rise with some Docklands regeneration that comes closest to a smaller Canary Wharf. Safety is strong (better than Croydon obviously, roughly Dublin-central at the Central-London level). Tech firm density: Stripe HQ, Susquehanna, Squarepoint, Fenergo, Workday, IBM Research, Intercom, plus EMEA HQs of nearly every Silicon Valley firm. **Verdict: strong and actionable.**

### 3b. North America

The North American story is dominated by the visa wall. Canada's points system punishes the profile. US H-1B is a 35% lottery. Mexico isn't really on the table for Caner's sectors. But the upside is so large that it demands real attention — a single successful punch-through is career-transforming.

The H-1B FY2026 (for work starting Oct 2025) selection rate was ~35%, up from ~25% in prior years because of USCIS's lottery-integrity reforms that cut duplicate registrations. The FY2027 lottery closed on 19 March 2026. **The structural reality is still that over half of qualified candidates lose the lottery every year, which means any US-first plan must be a multi-year plan with multiple attempts and an O-1 fallback.**

- **New York City** — Jane Street HQ, HRT HQ, Citadel Securities, Two Sigma, Tower Research, SIG, Virtu, DE Shaw, plus Anthropic NYC and Stripe NYC. Target firms with serious immigration teams: Citadel, Jane Street, HRT, Anthropic, Stripe, Databricks. Each of these either has internal immigration infrastructure that will put him in the lottery 2–3 years running or will prep an O-1A "extraordinary ability" alternative if the portfolio supports it.**

- **Seattle** — Microsoft, Amazon, Zillow, Databricks Seattle. Aesthetic is genuinely strong (Caner's profile anchors it as "strong"), weather is grey drizzle which he's fine with, safety is decent outside specific bad blocks. ## 4. Deep dive on the top 5

### Deep dive 1: London, UK

#### Tier 1 factors
- **Visa:** Already resolved for 16 months (Graduate visa until August 2027). The forcing question is whether he can convert to Skilled Worker sponsorship before the clock runs out. The 22 July 2025 salary threshold rise to £41,700 makes this harder for smaller fintechs but the target-tier firms (Jane Street, HRT, Citadel, Stripe, Cloudflare, Palantir, Thought Machine, Graphcore) routinely sponsor above £60k for new-grad SWEs and have dedicated immigration teams.
