# Interests and Hobbies

- **Strength training and hypertrophy** — currently running a [[Health/Fitness/Workouts/Full Body|Full Body rotation]] with 3 variants + a dedicated [[Health/Fitness/Workouts/Weakpoint Day|Weakpoint Day]]. 264 tracked sessions over 23 months documented in [[Health/Fitness/Overview|Fitness Overview]]; full training evolution and progression at [[Health/Fitness/Training Evolution]] and [[Health/Fitness/Progression]]. Genuinely interested in the underlying physiology, recovery science, and programming theory. Long-term aspiration is a **Third Space** membership in London once income allows — views it as one of the "modern establishments" worth investing in for both equipment quality and environment. See [[Profile/Lifestyle Preferences|Lifestyle Preferences]] for how gym access factors into city grading.
- **Boxing and Muay Thai** — training for practical self-defence, conditioning, and cardio rather than competition. Does not require a serious martial arts scene in a city to be happy there. *Not yet practised regularly; flagged as aspiration in [[Health/Cardio and Activity/Insights#activity-gap-analysis|Cardio Insights]].*
- **Working from cafés, coworking spaces, hotel lobbies, and libraries** — a core daily habit rather than an occasional thing. Large, air-conditioned, wifi-equipped cafés with plenty of seating are the preferred environment (Café Nero, Starbucks, Black Sheep Coffee). The background noise and ability to people-watch soothe ADHD/autism and aid focus rather than distract. This is the primary reason he bought a MacBook — portability and battery life to support café-based work sessions. See [[Profile/Lifestyle Preferences|Lifestyle Preferences]] for how café culture factors into city grading.
- **Evening and between-session walks** — used as decompression and a form of "mindful cardio" rather than exercise proper. Particularly enjoys regenerated urban greenery (Nine Elms, Kings Cross, York's riverside walks) for these.
- **Thunderstorms and harsh weather** — actively enjoys thunderstorms and misses them in London compared to Istanbul. Likes cold, snow, and dramatic weather generally. Not a factor in career decisions, but a real aesthetic preference.
- **Game modding** — 18+ released mods across RimWorld, Minecraft, Terraria, and Escape from Tarkov, totalling over 150,000 combined downloads. The original outlet for the same drive that now produces [[Profile/Projects|systems projects]]: understanding and extending complex software at a low level.
- **Cooking** — a genuine creative outlet, not a chore. Loves it, but pragmatic about time — effort scales with the day. On treat days: composed plates with 3-5 components, scratch sauces (chimichurri, fresh pesto, glazes), and deliberate presentation. On regular days: mince over rice, simple but well-seasoned. The core philosophy is **"homemade takeaway"** — recreating restaurant meals (burgers, ramen, steak dinners, pesto pasta) at home so he controls exact macros while eating food that tastes indulgent. This bridges the gap between tracking macros and actually enjoying food. Heavy seasoning is universal regardless of effort level (cracked pepper, chilli flakes, sesame seeds, fresh herbs, spring onions). Strong preference for charring and caramelisation. Dietary rule of thumb: every meal has a protein source, veg/fruit, and complex or simple carbs. Diet consists of protein, healthy fats, fruits, vegetables, and complex carbs — protein-dominant but no carb avoidance despite running a recomp cut. Go-to staples: tenderstem broccoli (veg), sweet potato (carb), chicken thighs and beef mince (protein). Buys high-protein versions of staples when available (protein milk, protein granola, protein bread rolls). Shops across Lidl, Sainsbury's, and M&S in London; see [[Health/Nutrition/Groceries/Overview|Groceries Tracking]] for the active cost-comparison project. Meal repertoire documented in [[Health/Nutrition/Meals|Meals]].
- **Traditional art** — drawing and painting as a creative practice.
- **Reading** — currently working through Camus, Kafka, and Dostoevsky. Broader interest in philosophy, scientific writing, and explanations that go a layer deeper than the surface.
- **Cross-disciplinary science** — mathematics, physics, biology, chemistry, particularly when concepts connect across domains.
- **Experiencing frontier technology as a user** — actively wants to live somewhere that gets Waymo-style AVs, robotaxis, cutting-edge medical AI, and other frontier tech early. This is separable from wanting to *build* frontier tech and should be treated as a lifestyle preference that shapes which cities make him feel "not left behind." See [[Profile/Lifestyle Preferences|Lifestyle Preferences]] for how this modulates city grading.
- **Housing and relocation** — London relocation is a major active domain. [[Flat Browser/Overview|Flat Browser]] is one of the most active projects — an engineering approach to flat-hunting with automated listing aggregation and scoring. [[Profile/Lifestyle Preferences|Lifestyle Preferences]] is one of the longest profile files, reflecting how seriously area selection, commute, and living environment are considered.
- **Job search and career strategy** — not just applying but engineering the process. [[Cernio/Overview|Cernio]] is the most data-rich project (408 companies, 1,184 jobs), treating the job search as a systems problem with structured pipelines, live dashboards, and AI-assisted matching.
- **AI agent design and skill engineering** — significant investment in how AI agents should be built and operated. Global configuration tracked under [[Projects/Claude Config/Overview|Claude Config]] (the `~/.claude/` repo, which retired the former `agent-skills` staging repo on 2026-04-19). Houses reusable skill architectures (feature pipelines, code health audits, context upkeep), plus the skill-creation research corpus packaged inside `skill-creator`.
