# Visa and Right to Work

## Current Visa / Immigration Status
UK Graduate visa

## Right to Work
Unrestricted right to work in the United Kingdom for the duration of the Graduate visa. No right to work elsewhere without sponsorship.

## Visa Expiry Date (if applicable)
August 2027

## Sponsorship Required?
Not currently. Skilled Worker visa sponsorship will be required from August 2027 onwards to continue working in the UK.

## Sponsorship Details
After August 2027, would require a Skilled Worker visa sponsored by a UK employer holding a valid sponsor licence. Strong preference for employers with a demonstrated, well-established Skilled Worker sponsorship track record.

## Security Clearance
None held. Not currently eligible for UK security clearance (SC, DV) due to nationality and residency requirements; may become eligible in the future after sustained UK residence.

## Notes
- Single nationality (Turkish), no dual citizenship.
- No pending applications.
- Current right to work is unrestricted within the UK until August 2027 with no employer sponsor required during this period, which makes the candidate inexpensive and low-friction to hire on a 0–2 year horizon.

## Mobility mindset
Globally mobile for the right opportunity. Proximity to Istanbul and family is not a constraint — explicitly views his early twenties as the time to build a career anywhere in the world, with the lifestyle and geography coming second to career trajectory. Willing to relocate outside the UK if the destination clears the visa and lifestyle tests (see [[Profile/Personal/Lifestyle Preferences|Lifestyle Preferences]] for the lifestyle filters).

Willing to learn a new language if a visa path requires it — French was cited specifically in the context of a guaranteed Canadian route. Currently at A2/B1 German (see [[Profile/Languages|Languages]]), which is an asset for Berlin / Munich / Zürich pathways.

Has a strong **integration mindset**: believes that relocating to a new country means adapting to its local culture rather than enforcing his own, and would actively put in the work to integrate rather than living in an expat bubble. This is an asset for destinations that reward integration (Germany, Japan, Netherlands culturally) as opposed to destinations that treat expats as a parallel population (Dubai, Singapore's expat-heavy enclaves).

## Hard exclusions arising from nationality
- **UK security clearance (SC / DV)** — not currently eligible due to nationality and residency requirements. Rules out Helsing UK, parts of Palantir UK, BAE Systems, and Faculty AI's Defence wing.
- **US security clearance roles** — same category, rules out US defence, intelligence-adjacent, and certain trading infrastructure roles.
- **German Bundeswehr / French DGSE-adjacent defence roles** — same category.
- **Canadian Express Entry without Canadian ties** — not a clearance issue, but a structural points-system bias: without Canadian education, Canadian work experience, or Canadian family, CRS scoring puts a fresh non-Canadian BEng in the 410-450 band, below the typical 470-500 cutoff. Possible via a provincial nominee route or an LMIA-backed job offer, but not realistic as a baseline plan.
